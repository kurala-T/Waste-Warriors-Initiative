Our Story & Team

Our Journey Begins: Harmony with Nature

"The smallest actions create the biggest change."

We are the students of Grade 7, and we have something very special planned — a project that began as a discussion during the Value in Focus – Harmony with Nature and has now grown into a school-wide movement!

We realized that living in harmony with nature means taking care of the world around us — not just in words, but through our daily actions. When we looked around, we noticed how much waste our school generates — paper bits, snack wrappers, chart scraps, and so much more. So, we decided to take action and become Waste Warriors, launching the Waste Warriors Initiative, W.W.I for short!

With the support of our teachers and housekeeping staff, we conducted a waste survey to understand what’s being thrown away and how we can do better. We learned that much of our waste can actually be reduced, reused, or recycled — if we start being more mindful in our classrooms.

Our mission is built on the **5Rs — Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Repurpose, and Recycle.** To bring this to life, we’re launching the “Clean Green Classroom Challenge”(CGCC) where every class will: Reduce waste generation, Segregate waste correctly, and Keep classrooms clean and eco-friendly.

And here’s an exciting addition — an Upcycling Challenge! Each class can create one artistic or useful item made entirely from classroom waste — like paper sculptures, eco-decor, or reusable organizers. This will show how creativity can transform waste into wonder!

Clean Green Classroom Challenge Results: Live Leaderboard

Classes of grades 6,7 and 8 are competing to be the Cleanest Greenest Classroom! Winners are chosen based on waste reduction, recycling accuracy, and innovative ideas.

Challenge Rules

Scoring Categories

  • Total Waste Reduction (from previous day): 30 pts
  • Paper Segregation: 10 pts
  • Creative Upcycled Works: 20 pts

Reducing Waste

  • Highest accuracy in sorting paper from general waste.
  • Greatest percentage decrease in total classroom waste.
  • Creative and practical ideas for reducing waste in school.

Upcycling

  • No using new materials.
  • Upcycling waste/extra materials (Creativity and usefulness).

Prizes: 1st, 2nd and 3rd place will get their Respective Position's Badge!
